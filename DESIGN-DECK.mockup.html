The scoping model (the "one agent → one surface" semantics)

You select…CenterDock · ReviewDock · ChatVoice + input route to
Agent (e.g. Freya) her workspace's pages — diffs against her branch/worktree, her screen, her terminal her workspace's ledger only your conversation with her her (voice-active)
Workspace (no agent) its pages + the review picker — agentless review works fully its ledger "no agent here" + the attach affordance unchanged (last active agent)
The one global exception is voice itself. The presence strip always tells the truth about sound: if Orion speaks while you're looking at Freya, his name and words show in the speaking slot (and his rail row gets the halo) — click the slot to jump to him. Sound is unscopeable; work is scoped. Rail chips (flagged / queued counts) also stay live for every agent, so nothing important goes dark.

Daemon support this revision adds (kept deliberately small)

  • Per-session user-input log — the chat tab's missing half. The agent half already exists (say_log, bounded deque of 50, persisted in the state dump). Symmetric addition: record dispatched transcripts on the session at dispatch() — {ts, text, origin: voice|typed, queued: bool}, same bound, same persistence. Not stored: route decisions and other moment data — the strip shows those live and they die with the moment. That's the whole complexity budget.
  • page.publish + workspace.open control commands (unchanged from rev 1) — agentless review parity.
  • speech.started/finished payloads gain who + text (unchanged from rev 1) — the speaking slot and rail halo need to know who says what.

Build order (unchanged shape, rev-2 content; each slice ends at your click-through)

  • U0 — protocol: page.publish, workspace.open, speech payload enrichment, per-session input log. Tests at the command seam before any pixel.
  • U1 — presence + scoping: strip (orb/captions/expansion card/one input/speaking slot), dock tabs Review|Chat with the scope header, feed strip + status bar + old chat tab removed. Checkpoint: you talk — you see it, long prompts readable; Freya talks — you see it; Orion's findings never appear under Freya.
  • U2 — review as a place: picker, repo Review buttons, ⊕ tab, the reference annotation editor, empty states, agentless flow. Checkpoint: diff-annotate parity, no agent running.
  • U3 — polish: settings modal, resizable persisted panels, type split, focus states, reduced-motion, light theme. Checkpoint: the "GitHub rando" walkthrough.

Still open — your call

  • Orb interaction: click cycles attention + hold = PTT (proposed), vs click = PTT toggle + attention via menu.
  • Input placement: top strip (proposed, shown) vs a single bottom bar. Exactly one exists either way.
  • Chat depth: is the bounded per-agent log (50 each side, restart-surviving) enough, or do you want a fuller durable transcript (bigger bound, export)? Lean: bounded — it's presence support, not a log system.
